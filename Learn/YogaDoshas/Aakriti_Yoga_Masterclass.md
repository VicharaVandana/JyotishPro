# Masterclass: Aakriti Yogas (Nabhasa Yogas)

## 1. Scriptural Reference & Formation Rules
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS) - Chapter 35 (Nabhasa Yogas), Shloka 1-5:**
> *“Asraya dala sankhyaakriti bhedena chaturbidha... Aakritiyoga vimsatihi...”*
> **Meaning:** Nabhasa Yogas are celestial patterns based solely on the distribution of all seven physical planets (excluding Nodes) across the 12 houses. **Aakriti (Figure/Shape) Yogas** are a subset of 20 specific geometric patterns formed by the planets. 
> *Examples:* Gada (Mace) when all planets are in two adjacent Kendras, Shakata (Cart) when all planets are in the 1st and 7th houses, Kamala (Lotus) when all planets are distributed across the four Kendras.

**B.V. Raman's "Three Hundred Important Combinations":**
Dr. Raman outlines that Nabhasa Yogas operate throughout a person's life (unlike Dashas which have specific timing). Aakriti Yogas imprint a "shape" or structural destiny onto the native. A "Gada" (Mace) makes a person destructive to enemies, while a "Kamala" (Lotus) makes them universally respected and pure.

## 2. Results & Effects
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS) - Specific Aakriti Results:**
Because there are 20 Aakriti Yogas, the results vary by shape:
- **Kamala (Lotus):** Highly virtuous, famous, lives a long life, commands respect.
- **Vajra (Thunderbolt):** Brave, fierce, wealthy in early and late life, faces struggles in middle age.
- **Pasha (Noose):** Earns wealth through restrictive or binding methods (like law enforcement or debt collection), can be cruel.

**Modern Synthesis:**
Aakriti Yogas define the "mold" of a person's life. If the planets form a balanced, symmetrical shape (like Kamala), the native's life is balanced and prosperous. If they form an extreme, rigid shape (like Shula/Spear, where all planets are in 3 houses), the native is highly specialized but lacks versatility and may have a volatile temper.

## 3. Cancellation/Mitigation (Bhanga)
- **Hierarchy of Yogas:** BPHS explicitly states a rule of hierarchy for Nabhasa Yogas. If an Aakriti Yoga forms simultaneously with an Aashraya, Dala, or Sankhya Yoga, the Aakriti Yoga is considered superior and cancels the others.
- **No Traditional Bhanga:** Since Nabhasa Yogas are based on the structural skeleton of the chart (the mere distribution of planets), they do not suffer from traditional Bhangas (like combustion or debilitation). A debilitated planet still occupies a house and completes the "shape."

## 4. Factors Modifying Strength
- **Strengthening Factors:** If the planets forming the 'points' of the geometric shape are in their own or exaltation signs, the positive traits of the Aakriti Yoga are amplified massively.
- **Weakening Factors:** If the shape is formed entirely by debilitated or combust planets, the native will physically resemble the psychological traits of the shape (e.g., rigid, angry, or expansive), but will lack the resources to succeed.

## 5. Prescribed Remedies
*Not applicable.* Nabhasa Yogas represent the indelible, foundational karma (Drida Karma) of the soul. They cannot be remedied, only understood and integrated.

## 6. Notes & Further Details
- **B.V. Raman's Observation:** Dr. Raman stresses that Nabhasa Yogas indicate the basic pattern of one's destiny. A person with an expansive Aakriti Yoga will never be content in a confined job, regardless of their current Dasha.
- **Scriptural Nuance:** The names of the Aakriti Yogas are deeply symbolic. For example, "Nauka" (Boat) Yoga forms when planets occupy all houses from the 1st to the 7th. A boat floats on water; hence, the native is always dependent on the flow of the masses (the public) for survival.
