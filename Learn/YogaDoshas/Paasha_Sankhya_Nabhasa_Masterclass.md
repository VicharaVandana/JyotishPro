# Masterclass: Paasha Sankhya Nabhasa Yoga

## 1. Scriptural Reference & Formation Rules
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS) - Chapter 35 (Nabhasa Yogas), Shloka 13-14:**
> *“Paasha Yoga is formed when the seven planets (from Sun to Saturn) are distributed in exactly five houses/signs in the birth chart.”*

**B.V. Raman's Observations:**
In his classical works, Dr. B.V. Raman categorizes Paasha Yoga under the **Sankhya Yogas** (Numerical Yogas), which are a subset of the **Nabhasa Yogas**. Sankhya Yogas depend entirely on the number of signs occupied by the seven visible planets (excluding Rahu and Ketu). Paasha translates to "noose," "rope," or "bondage."

**Formation Rules:**
- The seven main planets (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) must occupy exactly **five distinct signs** in the zodiac.
- Rahu and Ketu are explicitly **excluded** from this count.
- Sankhya Yogas are applicable only if no other major Nabhasa Yoga (like Akriti Yogas) is formed.

## 2. Results & Effects
**Phaladeepika - Chapter 6:**
> *“The native born with Paasha Yoga will be liable to be imprisoned, will be burdened with many expenses, will have a large family to support, but will be skillful in work and command many servants.”*

**General Interpretation:**
Because "Paasha" means a noose or bond, it indicates that the native's life may revolve around strong attachments, obligations, or responsibilities. The distribution of energy across five houses provides versatility, but the clustering also brings a sense of being tied down by worldly duties (like supporting a large family or managing many employees). 

## 3. Cancellation/Mitigation (Bhanga)
- **Overriding Yogas:** According to classical rules, Sankhya Yogas are general foundational yogas. If a more specific Nabhasa Yoga (like an Ashraya or Akriti Yoga) is formed by the same planetary distribution, the Sankhya Yoga (Paasha) is superseded and its specific negative effects (like bondage) are heavily mitigated or cancelled.
- **Dignity of Planets:** If the planets clustering in the 5 signs are in their own, exalted, or friendly signs, the "bondage" translates into positive responsibilities, such as running a large enterprise or being a beloved patriarch/matriarch.

## 4. Factors Modifying Strength
- **Strengthening Factors:** The yoga's effects are pronounced if the signs involved are fixed signs (Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius), which solidifies the "bondage" or fixed nature of the native's responsibilities.
- **Weakening Factors:** If the planets are situated in movable signs, the feeling of bondage is less restrictive, as the native frequently travels or changes environments, loosening the "noose."

## 5. Prescribed Remedies
If the native experiences the negative manifestations of this yoga (such as feelings of severe restriction, debt, or legal entanglement/incarceration), it is recommended to practice non-attachment (Vairagya) and engage in charitable acts. Releasing captive animals (like birds from cages) is a traditional remedy for Paasha Yoga.

## 6. Notes & Further Details
- **Nabhasa Yogas** are unique because they do not depend on Dashas (planetary periods) to manifest. Their effects are structural and felt throughout the native's entire life.
- The concentration of 7 planets into 5 signs means that at least two signs must have a conjunction (two planets), or one sign must have a stellium (three planets). This concentration creates intense focal points in the chart, anchoring the native's karma to those specific areas of life.
