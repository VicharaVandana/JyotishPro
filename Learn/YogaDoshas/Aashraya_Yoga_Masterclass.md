# Masterclass: Aashraya Yogas (Nabhasa Yogas)

## 1. Scriptural Reference & Formation Rules
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS) - Chapter 35 (Nabhasa Yogas), Shloka 6-7:**
> *“Kendreshu asraya yogah syat... Rajju, Musala, Nala bhedena trayah...”*
> **Meaning:** Aashraya Yogas (meaning "Refuge" or "Shelter") form when all seven physical planets take refuge in a specific modality of signs (Movable, Fixed, or Dual). There are three types:
> 1. **Rajju (Rope):** All planets in Movable signs (Aries, Cancer, Libra, Capricorn).
> 2. **Musala (Pestle):** All planets in Fixed signs (Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius).
> 3. **Nala (Reed):** All planets in Dual signs (Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces).

**B.V. Raman's "Three Hundred Important Combinations":**
Dr. Raman explains that Aashraya Yogas dominate the elemental and psychological modality of the native. A person with Rajju Yoga is constantly moving (like a swinging rope), Musala is immovably stubborn (like a heavy pestle), and Nala is constantly adapting (like a reed blowing in the wind).

## 2. Results & Effects
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS):**
- **Rajju Yoga (Movable):** The native is fond of wandering, highly ambitious, restless, resides in foreign lands, and is constantly seeking new ventures.
- **Musala Yoga (Fixed):** The native is wealthy, resolute, possesses immense perseverance, earns respect, is reliable, but can be deeply stubborn and resistant to change.
- **Nala Yoga (Dual):** The native is highly intelligent, physically attractive, adaptable, skilled in multiple trades, but may lack a firm sense of direction (indecisive).

## 3. Cancellation/Mitigation (Bhanga)
- **Aakriti Override:** As per BPHS rules, if an Aashraya Yoga forms, but the geometric distribution simultaneously creates an Aakriti Yoga (e.g., all planets in Movable signs, but they are all clustered in the 1st and 7th houses forming Shakata Yoga), the Aakriti Yoga overrides the Aashraya Yoga.
- **No Traditional Bhanga:** As structural yogas, they are not cancelled by combustion, debilitation, or malefic aspects. The psychological modality (Movable, Fixed, Dual) remains permanently etched into the native's character.

## 4. Factors Modifying Strength
- **Strengthening Factors:** The Yoga is purest when the Lagna (Ascendant) also falls into the same modality. For example, if a native has Musala Yoga (all planets in Fixed signs) and their Lagna is Leo (a Fixed sign), their perseverance and stability are legendary.
- **Weakening Factors:** If the planets are in Dual signs (Nala Yoga) but are heavily afflicted by malefics, the native's adaptability turns into deceit, instability, and a lack of loyalty.

## 5. Prescribed Remedies
*Not applicable.* This is a structural feature of the chart indicating foundational karma. 

## 6. Notes & Further Details
- **B.V. Raman's Observation:** Dr. Raman points out that Aashraya Yogas are rarely found in their absolute pure form (all 7 planets perfectly aligned). Even partial formations (5 or 6 planets) heavily tilt the native's life toward that specific modality.
- **Scriptural Nuance:** "Aashraya" means shelter. The soul has specifically chosen to take shelter in one type of energy for the entire incarnation. A Rajju native cannot be forced to sit still, and a Musala native cannot be forced to pivot quickly.
