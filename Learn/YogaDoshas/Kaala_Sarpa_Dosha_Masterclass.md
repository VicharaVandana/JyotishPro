# Kaala Sarpa Dosha Masterclass

## 1. Introduction
**Kaala Sarpa Dosha** is a highly debated but significant planetary alignment where all the main seven physical planets (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn) are hemmed between the shadow planets, **Rahu (North Node) and Ketu (South Node)**.

## 2. Formation Rules
The Dosha is formed when all planets lie strictly on one side of the Rahu-Ketu axis.
- **Anuloma (Direct):** Moving towards Rahu.
- **Viloma (Reverse):** Moving towards Ketu.
There are **12 specific types** of Kaala Sarpa Dosha based on the houses occupied by Rahu and Ketu (e.g., Ananta, Kulika, Vasuki, etc.).

## 3. Results & Effects
"Kaala" means time/death, and "Sarpa" means serpent. This dosha indicates a karmic blockage or extreme intensity.
- **Extremes in Life:** Native experiences extreme highs and lows. Success often comes after significant struggle, usually post the age of 33 or 42.
- **Intense Focus/Obsession:** The axis highlights the houses involved, creating an obsessive drive or inescapable karmic lessons in those areas.
- **Talent vs. Recognition:** The native may possess immense talent but face delays in getting due recognition.

## 4. Exceptions & Cancellations (Bhanga)
- If even one planet is completely outside the Rahu-Ketu axis, the Dosha is technically broken (though partial effects may remain).
- The presence of strong Raja Yogas or exalted planets can significantly mitigate the negative effects, turning the intense energy into massive success.

## 5. Remedies (Upayas)
- Worship of Lord Shiva (who controls the serpents) or Lord Ganesha.
- Chanting the Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra.
- Performing specific Kaala Sarpa Shanti Pujas at designated Jyotirlingas.
