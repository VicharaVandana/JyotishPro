# Masterclass: Sankhya Yogas (Nabhasa Yogas)

## 1. Scriptural Reference & Formation Rules
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS) - Chapter 35 (Nabhasa Yogas), Shloka 10-11:**
> *“Eka dwitrichatuh panchashatsapta gribhe sthitaih... Veena, Dama, Pasha, Kedara, Shula, Yuga, Gola...”*
> **Meaning:** Sankhya Yogas (Numerical Yogas) are determined entirely by the *number of houses* occupied by the seven physical planets (Sun to Saturn). There are seven variations:
> 1. **Gola (Globe):** All 7 planets confined to 1 house.
> 2. **Yuga (Yoke):** All 7 planets confined to 2 houses.
> 3. **Shula (Spear/Trident):** All 7 planets confined to 3 houses.
> 4. **Kedara (Field):** All 7 planets spread across 4 houses.
> 5. **Pasha (Noose):** All 7 planets spread across 5 houses.
> 6. **Dama (Wreath):** All 7 planets spread across 6 houses.
> 7. **Veena (Lute):** All 7 planets spread across all 7 separate houses.

**B.V. Raman's "Three Hundred Important Combinations":**
Dr. Raman states that Sankhya Yogas reveal the concentration of a person's life force. The fewer houses the planets occupy, the more hyper-focused, obsessive, and imbalanced the life becomes. The more houses they occupy, the more versatile, balanced, and harmonious the life is.

## 2. Results & Effects
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS):**
- **Gola (1 House):** Destitute, indolent, short-lived, wicked, rejected by society. (Too much concentrated energy causes implosion).
- **Yuga (2 Houses):** Poor, heretical, devoid of wealth and children.
- **Shula (3 Houses):** Brave, cruel, bears scars of battle, lacks wealth but has intense focus.
- **Kedara (4 Houses):** Wealthy, helpful to others, agriculturalist, steady.
- **Pasha (5 Houses):** Talkative, earns wealth through binding/restrictive means, slightly deceitful.
- **Dama (6 Houses):** Wealthy, famous, helps the public, possesses a balanced mind.
- **Veena (7 Houses):** Extremely wealthy, learned, musical, possesses all comforts, universally loved.

## 3. Cancellation/Mitigation (Bhanga)
- **Hierarchy Override:** This is the most crucial rule of Nabhasa Yogas. If a Sankhya Yoga forms, but the arrangement simultaneously creates an Aakriti, Aashraya, or Dala Yoga, the Sankhya Yoga is entirely cancelled and ignored. 
> *Example:* If all 7 planets are in 4 houses, it is Kedara Yoga. But if those 4 houses are specifically the 4 Kendras, it becomes *Kamala (Aakriti) Yoga*, which completely overrides Kedara. Therefore, Sankhya Yogas only apply if no other Nabhasa Yoga applies.

## 4. Factors Modifying Strength
- **Strengthening Factors:** For Veena, Dama, and Kedara Yogas (the beneficial ones), if the planets are placed in their exaltation, own signs, or friendly signs, the prosperity and versatility they grant are magnified immensely.
- **Weakening Factors:** For Gola, Yuga, and Shula Yogas, if the concentrated planets are heavily afflicted or combust (which is highly likely since the Sun is grouped with everyone else), the mental obsession and poverty are extremely severe.

## 5. Prescribed Remedies
*Not applicable.* Sankhya Yogas indicate the structural distribution of Prana (life force) in the native's destiny.

## 6. Notes & Further Details
- **B.V. Raman's Observation:** Dr. Raman advises astrologers not to apply Gola or Yuga yoga blindly. If the 2 houses involved in a Yuga Yoga are the 9th and 10th houses, the intense concentration creates an unparalleled Dharma-Karmadhipati Raja Yoga, yielding immense success despite the restrictive Sankhya classification.
- **Scriptural Nuance:** "Sankhya" means number. The ancient seers recognized that Nature favors balance. A 7-planet spread (Veena) creates beautiful harmony (like the 7 strings of a lute), whereas a 1-planet spread (Gola) creates a heavy, unmovable mass.
