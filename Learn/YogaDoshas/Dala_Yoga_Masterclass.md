# Masterclass: Dala Yogas (Nabhasa Yogas)

## 1. Scriptural Reference & Formation Rules
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS) - Chapter 35 (Nabhasa Yogas), Shlokas 8-9:**
> *“Dala yogau dwau mala sarpa sanjnakau...”*
> **Meaning:** Dala Yogas form when all benefics or all malefics are strictly relegated to Kendras (Angular houses: 1st, 4th, 7th, 10th). There are two types:
> 1. **Mala (Garland/Wreath) Yoga:** Formed when all three natural benefics (Jupiter, Venus, Mercury) occupy the Kendras, while the natural malefics are placed outside the Kendras.
> 2. **Sarpa (Serpent) Yoga:** Formed when all natural malefics (Sun, Mars, Saturn) occupy the Kendras, while the benefics are placed outside the Kendras. (Rahu and Ketu are generally excluded from Nabhasa calculations).

**B.V. Raman's "Three Hundred Important Combinations":**
Dr. Raman explains that Kendras represent the pillars of a person's life (Self, Home, Partner, Career). If these pillars are entirely supported by benefics, life is a beautiful garland (Mala). If the pillars are entirely occupied by malefics, life is a constricting serpent (Sarpa).

## 2. Results & Effects
**Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (BPHS):**
- **Mala Yoga:** The native will be consistently happy, endowed with conveyances, good clothes, and wealth. They will be highly educated, respected, and will enjoy a life of comfort and aesthetic pleasure.
- **Sarpa Yoga:** The native will be miserable, cruel, poor, dependent on others for food, and will constantly suffer from the hostility of the environment. 

**Phaladeepika - Chapter 6:**
Mantreswara echoes BPHS, emphasizing that Mala Yoga natives are "adorned by kings," whereas Sarpa Yoga natives are "venomous in speech and action, constantly facing grief."

## 3. Cancellation/Mitigation (Bhanga)
- **Aakriti Override:** If the arrangement also forms a specific geometric Aakriti Yoga, the Aakriti Yoga takes precedence in interpretation.
- **Benefic/Malefic Mixing:** If even one malefic enters a Kendra in Mala Yoga, or one benefic enters a Kendra in Sarpa Yoga, the Dala Yoga is technically cancelled and becomes an ordinary chart. This makes pure Dala Yogas exceptionally rare.

## 4. Factors Modifying Strength
- **Strengthening Factors:** In Mala Yoga, if Jupiter, Venus, and Mercury are in their own or exaltation signs within the Kendras (e.g., forming Hamsa, Malavya, and Bhadra Yogas simultaneously), the native attains legendary status, wealth, and spiritual purity.
- **Weakening Factors (For Sarpa Yoga):** If the malefics in the Kendras are exalted (e.g., Saturn in Libra in 1st, Mars in Capricorn in 4th), they form Panchamahapurusha Yogas (Sasa and Ruchaka). In this rare case, the "cruelty" of Sarpa Yoga is transformed into immense dictatorial, military, or executive power, though personal happiness may still be lacking.

## 5. Prescribed Remedies
- **For Sarpa Yoga:** The native must strictly avoid retaliatory anger. Daily worship of Lord Shiva (who controls serpents) and practicing selfless service can mitigate the immense environmental pressure caused by the malefics in the pillars of the chart.

## 6. Notes & Further Details
- **B.V. Raman's Observation:** Dr. Raman warns not to confuse Sarpa Yoga with Kaala Sarpa Dosha. Sarpa Yoga is purely about malefic planets occupying Kendras. It indicates a life where every major milestone (home, marriage, career) is a grueling battle.
- **Scriptural Nuance:** "Mala" represents beauty, celebration, and fragrance. It signifies that the native's mere presence brings joy to the environment.
